About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in English Literature at the University of Notre Dame, specializing in computational literary analysis, cognitive narratology and the novel. My obsession is fictional characters. My dissertation offers a new theory of percieved character realism by applying insights from social psychology and computational analysis alongside traditional literary research methods to explain why behaviour descriptions make us think about characters like real people.

I am dedicated to fostering interdisciplinary dialogue, collaboration and research in the academy. To this end, in 2020 I founded Cyborg, a guest speaker and seminar series at the University of Notre Dame focused on interdisciplinary research approaches that cross STEM/humanities boundaries. Currently, I am a Distinguished Graduate Fellow at the Notre Dame Institute for Advanced Study, Notre Dame's primary interdisciplinary research center.

I am particularly interested in developing new digital methods for studying characterization in written fiction, and I am delighted to hear from potential collaborators on this topic.
